# OverSee User Manual: Family Digital Safety

OverSee is a parental monitoring application designed to provide digital safety for children by monitoring activity on social media platforms like **Facebook** and **Instagram**. It uses advanced text analysis and end-to-end encryption to alert parents to inappropriate content while maintaining strict data privacy.

---

## 1. Getting Started

### Account Creation
1.  **Register**: Open the app and select **Sign Up**. Enter your name, email, and a secure password.
2.  **Terms of Service**: You must review and accept the Privacy Policy and User Agreement, which outlines how your family's data is encrypted and handled.
3.  **Security Warning**: Your password is the key to your child's encrypted data. **If you reset your password, all historical logs will be permanently deleted** for privacy reasons.

### Role Selection
After logging in, you must designate the device's role:
* **Parent Device**: Used to monitor activity, manage settings, and receive alerts.
* **Child Device**: Used for background monitoring and protection on the child's phone.

---

## 2. Setting Up the Child Device

To begin monitoring, the child's device requires specific permissions and a secure link to the parent account.

### Linking the Devices
1.  **Obtain ID**: On the Child Device, a unique **6-digit ID** will be displayed.
2.  **Enter ID**: On the Parent Device, navigate to the **Overview** tab and select **Link Child Device**. Enter the 6-digit ID shown on the child's phone.
3.  **Transferring Devices**: If you link a new child device to an existing account, the app will prompt you to transfer the link and securely delete old logs from the previous device.

### Required Permissions
For monitoring to function, the following must be enabled on the child's device via the **Service Status** indicators:
* **Accessibility**: Allows the app to detect when Facebook or Instagram is opened.
* **Overlay**: Enables the app to display security warnings or block access during "Penalty Timeouts".
* **Screen Capture**: Required for the app to analyze text on the screen for safety.
* **Notifications & Internet**: Necessary for syncing data and sending alerts to the parent.

### Child Dashboard & Smart PIN
* **Smart PIN**: During setup, you must create a **4-digit PIN**. This PIN is required to access the Child Dashboard or change settings on the child's phone, preventing unauthorized tampering.
* **Status Check**: If any service icon on the Child Dashboard turns **Red**, tap it to be taken directly to the system settings to fix the permission.

---

## 3. Parent Dashboard Features

### Overview Tab
* **Activity Trend**: A visual chart showing incident frequency over the selected date range.
* **Summary Cards**: Quickly view "Peak Activity" times and the total number of reports.
* **Top Flagged Words**: See the most frequently detected inappropriate words.

### Daily Insights
* **Activity Heatmap**: A timeline showing exactly when incidents occurred throughout the day.
* **Detected Content**: A list of specific incidents for the day, sorted by severity.

### Incident History (Logs)
* **Detailed Records**: View the **Raw Word** detected, the **Matched Word** (what the system identified it as), the **App** it was found in, and the exact **Time**.
* **Severity Levels**: Incidents are categorized as **Low**, **Medium**, or **High** risk.

---

## 4. Safety Mechanisms

### Automated Alerts
When a **High Severity** incident is detected, the app performs the following:
1.  **Instant Sync**: Immediately uploads the log to the parent's dashboard.
2.  **Push Notification**: Sends an emergency alert to the parent's phone.

### Penalty Timeouts
If the system detects a "High-Risk Burst" (too many flags in a short period), it may trigger a **Penalty Timeout**.
* The child will be blocked from the monitored app (e.g., Facebook) for a set duration (default is 5 minutes).
* A countdown timer will appear on the child's screen. They must wait for the timer to end or exit the app.

---

## 5. Security & Privacy

* **End-to-End Encryption (E2EE)**: All monitoring data is encrypted directly on the child's device using a unique key before being sent to the cloud.
* **Zero-Knowledge**: Because the data is encrypted with your account password, **OverSee employees cannot read your child's logs**.
* **Data Control**: Parents can export all data as a CSV/PDF or permanently delete the account and all associated logs at any time.
